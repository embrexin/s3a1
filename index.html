The grand drawing room of the Foster Manor, where an evening of celebration has turned dark.

A storm rages outside, lightning flashing through the tall windows.

Maggie Foster, the young heiress, stands at the center of attention, her innocence and wealth drawing envy, loyalty, and desire.

Around her are Elena Rivera, a jilted socialite; Nina Patel, an observant researcher with secrets; Liam Brooks, her former lover now chasing Maggie’s fortune; and Arthur King, the protective butler who serves the Foster family.
The evening begins with everyone raising a glass to Maggie, who smiles, blissfully unaware of the dangerous alliances and hidden motives surrounding her.

Maggie Foster

(cheerfully)

To all of us! Here’s to friendship and trust
The others exchange loaded glances. Elena shifts uncomfortably, clutching her glass a bit too tightly.

Elena Rivera

(forcing a smile)

Trust. Yes… that’s what we’re all here for, isn’t it?
Liam leans in closer to Maggie, a subtle but unsettling shift in his demeanor.

Liam Brooks

(whispering)

I’d like to think you trust me, Maggie. We share… common interests.
Arthur watches Liam carefully, a flicker of distrust in his eyes.

Arthur King

(calmly)

Miss Maggie, it’s important to know who’s truly loyal. Wealth, after all, can bring out the worst in people.

Nina Patel

(almost to herself, studying each person)

Yes, and sometimes loyalty goes unnoticed until it’s too late.

(pauses)

I've been keeping a close eye on everyone here. And I must say, I’m quite the observer.
Everyone goes silent, the atmosphere tense as Maggie frowns, sensing a depth to the room’s silence that she hadn’t noticed before.
The room is dark, save for the occasional flash of lightning. Arthur leaves briefly to check the fuse box after a surge cuts the lights, leaving the group in dim candlelight. The shadows deepen, tension palpable.

Elena Rivera

(whispering to Maggie, bitterness barely concealed)

You should know, Maggie, people don’t always have your best interests at heart.

(glancing at Liam)

Some just take whatever they want.

Maggie Foster

(nervous)

What do you mean? I thought we were all friends here.

Liam Brooks

(smiling too widely)

Of course, we are, Maggie.

(pauses, glancing at Elena)

Some just have trouble letting go of the past.
Nina reaches into her bag, pulling out a small notebook, and flips through its pages, reading silently. She mutters under her breath.

Nina Patel

(thoughtfully)

It’s all here… I’ve kept track of everything—whispers, lies, jealous glances.

(looks at Maggie)

You’d be surprised what I’ve uncovered.
Arthur re-enters, his face grave

Arthur King

The storm’s made it difficult to keep the lights steady. I’d advise staying close.
Maggie, growing visibly uncomfortable, catches sight of a small vial in Arthur’s pocket as he leans down to fix the fireplace.

Maggie Foster

(concerned)

Arthur… what is that?
Arthur quickly tucks it away, his expression hardening.

Arthur King

Merely something for emergencies, Miss Maggie. Nothing you need to worry about.
Nina’s eyes narrow, sensing something off. She makes a note, eyeing the others carefully.
Suddenly, there’s a scream. Maggie clutches her throat, gasping for air as she stumbles backward, her glass dropping to the floor. She collapses, lifeless, as the room falls silent. The remaining four stand frozen in horror.

Arthur King

(rushing to her side, desperate)

Miss Maggie!

(feeling for a pulse, then looking up, grim)

She’s gone.
Everyone is silent, the gravity of the moment settling in

Elena Rivera

(recovering, voice shaking)

Someone here did this.

(eyes dart to Liam)

You were the last one with her, whispering sweet nothings. How far would you go for her fortune?

Liam Brooks

(defensive)

Look who’s talking! You’ve hated Maggie since you found out I’d moved on.

(glaring at Elena)

Maybe this was your twisted way of revenge.
Nina steps forward, holding up her notebook.

Nina Patel

Enough. I have records of all your secrets.

(opens a page, reading)

Elena, your resentment toward Maggie has been simmering for months.

And Arthur… that vial you carry. Care to explain?

Arthur King

(coldly)

I protect this family.

That vial was a simple sedative, in case of emergency.

If I’d wanted harm, I wouldn’t have needed to be so subtle.
Nina flips another page, addressing Liam.

Nina Patel

(accusingly)

Liam, you left your last relationship abruptly, and Maggie’s fortune seemed awfully convenient, didn’t it?
The room is silent, each suspect staring at one another, weighed down by accusation and guilt.
Choose who you think killed the young heiress...

Elena Rivera

Elena finally breaks down, admitting that her jealousy and anger led her to poison Maggie’s drink when no one was looking.

She wanted to see Maggie’s perfect life crumble just as hers had. As Arthur calls the authorities, Elena lets out a bitter laugh, feeling both defeated and victorious.

Liam Brooks

Liam’s façade cracks as Nina’s notes expose his true motives. In a desperate attempt to secure Maggie’s fortune, he slipped something into her drink, believing he could inherit her wealth.

When Arthur restrains him, he sneers, unrepentant, revealing that his love for Maggie had always been a game.

Arthur King

Arthur’s composure falters, revealing his twisted loyalty.

He admits he poisoned Maggie, believing her fortune was a curse that drew selfish people into her life. He thought removing her would protect the Foster name from greed.

The others look on in shock as Arthur is taken away, his loyalty now seen as a dark, fatal obsession.
As the guilty party is led away, the remaining suspects are left in the shadowy silence of the Foster Manor, haunted by their secrets and the memory of Maggie’s tragic end.
